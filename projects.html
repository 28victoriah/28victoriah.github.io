Research

Optimized pathway for designing small molecules with a hybrid quantum classical generative adversarial network.

Abstract: The modern process of drug discovery and development requires large financial investment and time, which is why researchers are utilizing new computational chemistry methods like machine learning to speed up molecular synthesis pathways. Alzheimer's disease is a neurodegenerative disease associated with the aggregation of many proteins, including butyrylcholinesterase (BChE). The treatment requires molecules that can penetrate the extremely selective blood-brain barrier (BBB) and inhibit BChE. Our model architecture utilizes a Wasserstein generative adversarial network (GAN) with a quantum generator, classical discriminator, and a gradient penalty. It was trained selectively on inhibitors for BChE from the IPAD-DB database of Alzheimer's protein inhibitors. Post processing algorithms then checked for size, lipophilicity, planarity, aromatic rings, as well as other functional groups critical for determining effective molecules that target BChE. Out of complete generation of 100 molecules, 100% were small, 89% had amine groups, 30% passed BBB permeability checkers, and 93% were predicted to have low aggregation potential. From molecular docking, 97% were able to interact, with the binding affinity between the main structure of the generated inhibitors to BCHE ranging -2.8 to -4.4, indicating weak binding capability. This approach offers the framework to accelerate the discovery of promising therapeutics for Alzheimer's disease by shortening the amount of time needed in the lab to find a molecule with optimized properties for BBB penetration and BChE inhibition.

Summer Math Workshop

Goal: The goal of this program is to strengthen problem solving skills by using mathematics that goes beyond memorization to conceptual understanding, increase student interest for mathematics with fun and engaging workshops, boost collaboration, and emphasize the importance of learning by allowing students to embrace and learn from their mistakes.

Topics:

Workshop #1: Multiples and Divisibility

Workshop #2: Sequences

Workshop #3: Series

Workshop #4: Counting

Workshop #5: Probability

Workshop #6: Geometry

Style: This program is held over a series of workshop topics at the San Jose Public Library Bascom branch, and each workshop is approximately 30 mintues long.
